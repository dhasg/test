About the Directorate

The Asuogyaman District Health Directorate operates under the Ghana Health Service and is responsible for planning, coordinating, implementing, and monitoring healthcare delivery in the district.

Our Vision

A healthy and productive population in Asuogyaman District.

Our Mission

To provide accessible, equitable, and quality healthcare services through effective resource utilization and strong community partnerships.

Asuogyaman District Profile

The Asuogyaman District, located in the Eastern Region of Ghana, is known for its scenic beauty, agriculture, and health infrastructure.

Introduction

The Asuogyaman District is located in the Eastern Region of Ghana, covering an area of 2,236.2 square kilometers. The district is home to about 100,000 residents. It is known for its diverse cultures, natural resources, and economic activities. Volta Lake, a major body of water in the region, is a prominent feature of the district.

Key Health Information

The district has several health facilities that serve the population. These facilities provide primary healthcare services, maternal and child healthcare, and disease prevention programs. The district health directorate also runs immunization and family planning services.

  • Health Facilities: 15 health centers and clinics
  • Population Coverage: 100,000 residents
  • Maternal Health Program: Reducing maternal mortality through outreach and education
  • Child Health Services: Immunization programs, pediatric care, and health education

District Map

Key District Features

  • Volta Lake: A major water body used for transportation and fishing activities.
  • Agriculture: Known for cocoa, maize, and vegetable farming. The district also produces timber and fish.
  • Cultural Heritage: The district celebrates several annual festivals and has a rich cultural heritage.
  • Road Infrastructure: Well-developed roads linking the district to nearby towns and the capital.

Challenges and Opportunities

The Asuogyaman District faces challenges such as limited access to healthcare in remote areas, inadequate infrastructure, and environmental issues. However, there are significant opportunities for growth, particularly in the areas of agriculture and tourism.

  • Challenges:
    • Limited access to healthcare in remote communities
    • Inadequate road infrastructure in rural areas
    • Environmental issues affecting agriculture and fishing
  • Opportunities:
    • Tourism development around Volta Lake and cultural sites
    • Expansion of agriculture and improved farming techniques
    • Improvement in road infrastructure for better connectivity

Conclusion

The Asuogyaman District has the potential to grow into a more prosperous and sustainable region, with a strong focus on agriculture, healthcare, and tourism. With proper investment and infrastructural development, it can overcome its challenges and tap into its many opportunities.
