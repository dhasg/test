Asuogyaman District Profile

An overview of the district's health services and developments.

Introduction

The Asuogyaman District is located in the Eastern Region of Ghana. The district covers an area of 2,236.2 square kilometers and has a population of approximately 100,000 residents. The district is known for its scenic landscapes, including the Volta Lake, which plays an important role in its economy and culture.

Key Health Information

The district has a network of health facilities that provide essential health services to the community. The Asuogyaman District Health Directorate oversees the provision of healthcare, with an emphasis on maternal and child health, disease prevention, and community health education.

  • Health Facilities: 12 health centers and clinics
  • Population Coverage: 100,000 residents
  • Maternal Health Program: Focus on reducing maternal mortality
  • Child Health Services: Immunization programs and pediatric care

Key District Features

The district has several unique features that make it an important area for both development and tourism.

  • Volta Lake: A major water body contributing to the local economy
  • Agriculture: The district is known for its agricultural activities, including cocoa and maize farming
  • Cultural Sites: The district has a rich cultural heritage, with several traditional festivals celebrated annually
  • Road Infrastructure: Well-connected road systems for accessibility

Challenges and Opportunities

The district faces several challenges but also has many opportunities for growth and development.

  • Challenges:
    • Poor road infrastructure in rural areas
    • Limited access to healthcare in remote communities
    • Environmental issues affecting agriculture
  • Opportunities:
    • Expansion of healthcare services and infrastructure
    • Tourism development around Volta Lake
    • Improvement in agricultural practices and yield

Conclusion

The Asuogyaman District, despite its challenges, offers immense potential for growth in various sectors, including health, agriculture, and tourism. With continuous efforts from local authorities and partners, the district is poised for a brighter future.
